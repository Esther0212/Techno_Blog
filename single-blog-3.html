Unpacking the Business Model Canvas for Our Pet Adoption App

  • By Esther Beuthel Eblacas
  • October 24, 2024

Customer Segments:

The app primarily targets pet owners, adopters, and animal rescue organizations, working to connect these groups for a better adoption experience and building a stronger pet adoption ecosystem.

Channels:

The platform reaches its audience via social media, mobile app stores, partnerships with pet organizations, and LGUs to maximize visibility and accessibility.

Customer Relationships:

Relationships are nurtured through pet supply freebies, charitable donations, and fostering online communities to build trust and maintain user engagement.

Value Proposition:

The app provides a streamlined adoption process, allowing users to browse available pets for adoption. It offers personalized matches based on preferences, lifestyle, and compatibility between pets and adopters.

Key Resources:

The main resources include veterinarians, database administrators, mobile app developers, and social workers, ensuring smooth operations, data management, and community support.

Key Activities:

Core activities include creating and maintaining the app, marketing, online promotion, communicating with customer support, and app maintenance. These tasks are crucial for the platform's ongoing functionality and user engagement.

Key Partners:

The main partners identified are local government units (LGUs), CDO Animal Welfare Society, City Veterinary Office, and Provincial Veterinary Office. These partners help in building credibility and extending the platform’s influence in the community.

Cost Structure:

The key costs include app development, deployment, monthly expenses for pet supplies, labor costs, and maintenance costs, which are necessary to keep the platform running efficiently.

Revenue Streams:

Revenue will be generated through ad revenues, sponsorships, and commissions on successful adoptions, providing a sustainable financial model for the app.



Personal Viewpoint:

Working through the Business Model Canvas for the pet adoption app has enhanced my understanding of how business elements contribute to sustainability. I learned that app development requires forming partnerships with local organizations, balancing technical work with community engagement, and leveraging revenue streams like ads and sponsorships. This process has increased my confidence in the business side of app development, emphasizing that a strong strategy is as important as the product itself.
